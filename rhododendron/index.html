Pacific Rhododendron

Common alternate names

  • Pacific rhododendron
  • California rosebay
  • California rhododendron
  • Coast rhododendron
  • big leaf rhododendron
  • Rhododendron macrophyllum

Physical size range

  • Height: 2–9 m (7–30 ft) tall
  • Leaves: 7–23 cm (3–9 in) long, 3–7 cm (1–3 in) broad
  • Flowers: 2.8–4 cm (1.1–1.6 in) long

Geographic range

Native to the Pacific Coast of North America, from northern British Columbia (Canada) to Monterey Bay (California). Commonly found in the Coast Mountains and Cascade Range, with populations in coastal Washington, northern Oregon, and extending eastward into the Cascades in Oregon, Washington, and British Columbia.

Preferred climate and habitat

Thrives in temperate, moist climates of the Pacific Northwest. Grows from sea level up to 1,800 m (6,000 ft) elevation. Prefers disturbed habitats (e.g., roadsides, recently cleared forests), acidic or well-drained soils, partial shade to full sun, and moderate to high rainfall. Evergreen shrub adapted to coastal fog and cool, wet winters with mild summers.
